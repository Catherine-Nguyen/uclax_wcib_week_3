Glass Engraving Technique

Disclaimer: For week 3 of Frontend Web Coding Bootcamp at UCLA Extension, I will create an instructional web page for glass engraving technique. I would like to credit Johana from JJ Paperie & Co for this amazing tutorial and Katerina Dima from Only Deco Love for the amazing background photo.

Introduction

Follow Johana's step-by-step to create a personalized gift for your loved ones! If you have experience writing in Copperplate or Spencerian, you can even engrave in calligraphy!

Supplies

  • Engraver
  • White Pencil
  • Rub-N-Buff
  • Old brush for applying Rub-n-Buff
  • Old brush for dusting off any glass residue
  • Cloth
  • Safety Glasses
  • Mask

Instructions

Step 1: Sketch your lettering with white pencil.

Step 2: Start engraving with slow speed and slowly increase it based on your comfort level. Something important to remember is that not all surfaces are smooth!

Step 3: Apply more strokes on downward lines of each letter to create a faxu calligraphy effect. Remember upward strokes are thin lines and downward strokes are thick lines.

Step 4: Use your brush to brush off all residue after engraving. Examine your work and do minor touch-up if needed.

Step 5: Apply a small amount of rub-n-buff on your lettering. Remember to do this step quickly as rub-n-buff does dry quickly.

Step 6: Grab your cloth and start wiping off the extra rub-n-buff on your engraving. Repeat this step for every word.
